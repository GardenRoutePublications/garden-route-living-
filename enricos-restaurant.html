Enrico's: Where the Ocean Meets the Table

Three decades of legendary sunsets, fresh catch, and the famous Bomba at Keurboomstrand.

By Garden Route Living

There are restaurants with ocean views, and then there's Enrico's—where the ocean isn't just scenery, it's the source of what lands on your plate.

Perched on the rocks at Keurboomstrand, a few kilometres east of Plettenberg Bay, Ristorante Enrico has been the Garden Route's defining dining experience for over thirty years. The formula sounds simple: catch fish in the morning, serve it that evening, repeat. But simplicity is the hardest thing to get right.

The restaurant operates its own fishing boat, the Enrico, which heads out at dawn most mornings to work the waters off Plett. What comes back—linefish, crayfish, prawns—becomes that day's specials. There's no freezer full of backup supplies here. When the sea is too rough to fish, the menu adapts.

The Bomba

Every regular knows about the Bomba. This seafood platter for two has achieved almost mythical status along the Garden Route—a towering arrangement of whatever the ocean offered that day, prepared simply to let the freshness speak for itself.

"We don't have a freezer full of backup supplies. When the sea is too rough to fish, the menu adapts."

The Setting

Tables spill out onto the wooden deck, positioning diners just metres from where waves crash against ancient rocks. In summer, the sunset light turns the scene golden; in winter, storms provide dinner theatre of the most dramatic kind.

It's this combination—food that couldn't be simpler or fresher, in a setting that couldn't be more spectacular—that has made Enrico's a genuine Garden Route institution.

Visiting

Enrico's is located on Keurboomstrand Beach Road, between Plettenberg Bay and The Crags. Booking is essential, especially for sunset tables in peak season.

For reservations, visit enricos.co.za or call the restaurant directly.
