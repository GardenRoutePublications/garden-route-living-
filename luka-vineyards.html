Luka Vineyards

Where wine meets wilderness in the Crags, outside Plettenberg Bay.

By Garden Route Living

The Garden Route isn't wine country in the traditional sense. But at Luka Vineyards, nestled in the hills above Plettenberg Bay, that's precisely the point.

Luka is an experiment in terroir—proof that interesting wine can come from unexpected places. The combination of coastal influence, altitude, and the region's unique microclimate produces something genuinely different from the established winelands further west.

The Setting

The tasting room looks out over paddocks where horses graze against a backdrop of the Tsitsikamma mountains. It's a working farm as much as a winery, and that unpretentious, agricultural character defines the experience. You're not visiting a manicured estate—you're seeing where wine actually gets made.

The Wines

The focus is on small-batch, handcrafted wines that reflect the specific character of this corner of the Garden Route. Production is limited, and the wines often sell out. If you find something you like, buy it—it may not be there next time.

Visiting

Luka is open for tastings and cellar tours by appointment. The setting makes it worth combining with lunch—platters and light meals are available on the deck, with views that rival any in the winelands.

Located in The Crags, approximately 20 minutes from Plettenberg Bay.
