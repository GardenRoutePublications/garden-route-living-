Our Story

About Us

We're a small team of writers, photographers and storytellers who happen to live in the most beautiful corner of South Africa. Garden Route Living is our love letter to this coastline.

It started, as these things often do, over wine. A few friends sitting on a deck in Knysna, watching the sun drop behind the Heads, talking about how many incredible stories go untold along this stretch of coast. The artisan cheesemaker in Rheenendal. The third-generation oyster farmer. The architect reimagining what coastal living means. The trail runner who knows every hidden path through the Tsitsikamma.

We realised that while tourists flock here for the scenery, what makes the Garden Route special is its people. The makers and dreamers who choose this place. The families who've been here for generations. The newcomers who arrive for a holiday and never quite leave.

Garden Route Living launched in 2026 with a simple mission: tell the stories that matter to the people who live here, and share the best of this region with those who love it from afar. We're not interested in listicles or clickbait. We believe in slow journalism, beautiful photography, and giving subjects the space they deserve.

Our coverage spans from Mossel Bay to Plettenberg Bay and everywhere in between: George, Wilderness, Sedgefield, Knysna, The Crags, Nature's Valley. We write about food and wine, design and architecture, adventure and wellness, property and business. But mostly, we write about people.

We're proudly independent. No corporate owners, no investors demanding growth at all costs. Just a small team doing work we believe in, supported by local advertisers who share our values and readers who appreciate what we do.

"The Garden Route isn't just where we work. It's where we swim at dawn, where our kids go to school, where we know the barista by name. This is home."

— The Garden Route Living Team

What We Believe

We believe local journalism matters. That communities thrive when their stories are told with care. That the best writing comes from people who actually live in a place, who understand its rhythms and contradictions, who have skin in the game.

We believe in celebrating what's good without ignoring what's complicated. The Garden Route faces real challenges: water scarcity, fire risk, economic inequality, the tension between development and conservation. We don't shy away from these conversations.

We believe beautiful design elevates everything. That a well-crafted article deserves a well-crafted presentation. That reading should be a pleasure, not a chore.

Most of all, we believe this coastline is worth caring about. Worth documenting, worth protecting, worth sharing with the world.

The Team

Meet the Locals

A small crew with deep roots along the coast

Sarah van der Berg

Founder & Editor

Former features editor at a Cape Town daily, Sarah traded deadlines for tides when she moved to Knysna in 2019. She can be found most mornings swimming at Leisure Isle before the rest of the town wakes up.

James Fortuin

Senior Writer

Born in George, educated in Stellenbosch, returned to the Garden Route because nowhere else felt right. James covers food, wine, and the people behind both. His espresso habit funds half the cafés in town.

Lindi Mkhize

Photographer

Lindi's camera has taken her from Joburg fashion weeks to Plett sunrises. She moved south for a six-month break in 2021 and forgot to leave. Specialises in making everyone look their best, including the mountains.

Michael Chen

Digital & Design

A Wilderness local since his parents opened a guesthouse here in the '90s, Michael handles everything digital. When he's not designing, he's probably paddling the Touw River or building something in his garage.

Anke Joubert

Contributing Writer

Anke writes about property, architecture, and interior design from her home in Sedgefield. She knows every house on the market and half the architects personally. Her golden retriever, Biscuit, is a local celebrity.

Thabo Ndlovu

Adventure Editor

If it involves mountains, waves, or trails, Thabo's written about it. A qualified mountain guide and former Otter Trail ranger, he now covers outdoor pursuits from his base in The Crags. Yes, he's done the bungee. Twice.
