The Workshop Interiors

How a Knysna design studio became the Garden Route's go-to for considered coastal living.

By Garden Route Living

Interior design on the Garden Route faces unique challenges. Salt air, humidity, intense UV, and the ever-present risk of mould mean that choices made for Johannesburg or Cape Town simply don't translate.

The Workshop Interiors has built its reputation on understanding these challenges—and solving them with style. Based in Knysna, the studio combines practical coastal expertise with a refined aesthetic sensibility that's earned them a devoted following.

The Approach

Founder Tanya believes in what she calls "considered living"—spaces that work beautifully for how people actually live, not just how rooms photograph. On the Garden Route, that means thinking hard about maintenance, durability, and the way light moves through a space across seasons.

"We design for real life. How a room photographs matters less than how it feels at 6pm with a glass of wine."

The Showroom

The Workshop's Knysna showroom functions as both retail space and design consultation studio. Carefully curated furniture, textiles, and accessories sit alongside custom pieces made by local craftspeople. It's a working space—you'll often find Tanya meeting with clients, fabric samples spread across the central table.

The Process

Projects typically begin with a home visit, where the team assesses the specific conditions of the site: orientation, exposure, existing finishes. From there, they develop schemes that balance client preferences with practical realities. The result is interiors that feel both beautiful and resilient—designed for the Garden Route rather than despite it.
