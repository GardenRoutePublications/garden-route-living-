The Workshop Interiors: A Family Legacy in Design

For twenty-one years, a mother and daughter have been quietly shaping how the Garden Route lives. This is the story of Tanya and Fransie Mans.

By Garden Route Living

On the corner of Main and Nelson Street in Knysna, where the morning light filters through milkwood trees, there exists a particular kind of magic. It happens in the space between a client's hesitant description of what they want and the moment they see it realised—transformed from words into fabric, timber, and light.

The Workshop Interiors has been performing this quiet alchemy since 2004, when Tanya Mans joined her mother Fransie in what would become one of the Garden Route's most enduring design practices. Twenty-one years later, the mother-daughter partnership has shaped hundreds of homes along this coastline, each one a testament to their shared conviction: that good design is not about following trends, but about understanding lives.

"Nothing is too big or too small," they say, and they mean it. A single set of curtains receives the same considered attention as a complete home transformation. The clients who return, year after year, do so because they've experienced what that attention feels like.

The Language of Place

Working on the Garden Route demands a particular literacy. Salt air ages fabric differently than the highveld sun. Humidity warps timber that would stay true in Johannesburg. The quality of coastal light—that luminous, ever-shifting reflection off water—requires its own palette, its own textures.

Tanya and Fransie have spent two decades learning this language, translating it into spaces that feel inevitable rather than imposed. Their work doesn't announce itself. It simply belongs—to the house, to the landscape, to the people who live there.

"Good design doesn't shout. It settles into a space like it was always meant to be there."

Craftsmanship as Philosophy

In an era of flat-pack furniture and disposable décor, The Workshop Interiors represents something increasingly rare: proper craftsmanship. Their headboards are built to last generations. Their upholstery ages with grace. The cushions scattered across their showroom floor aren't mere accessories—they're small declarations of belief in things made well.

This commitment to quality runs deeper than aesthetics. It's rooted in the understanding that a home isn't a showroom—it's where children spill juice, where dogs commandeer sofas, where life happens in all its beautiful mess. The Workshop's designs don't just survive this reality; they're made for it.

The Family Dynamic

There's something particular about working with family. The shorthand that develops. The trust that doesn't need to be earned because it was inherited. Tanya learned at Fransie's side, absorbing not just technique but philosophy—the way her mother moves through a space, reading its possibilities.

Now, two decades in, they work as a single sensibility expressed through two perspectives. Fransie brings the wisdom of experience; Tanya, the energy of evolution. Together, they navigate the tension between tradition and contemporary living that defines Garden Route design.

An Enduring Presence

Knysna has changed enormously since 2004. Sleepy forest town has become sought-after coastal destination. Property prices have climbed. New residents have arrived with new expectations. Through it all, The Workshop Interiors has remained—adapting, certainly, but never abandoning the principles that made them trusted in the first place.

Their showroom on Main Street is worth visiting even if you're not currently planning a project. The space itself is a demonstration of their approach: curated rather than crowded, warm rather than clinical, inviting rather than intimidating. It feels like someone's very tasteful home, which is precisely the point.
