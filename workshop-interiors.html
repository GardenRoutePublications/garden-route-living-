The Workshop Interiors: Two Decades of Garden Route Design

From their corner showroom on Main Road, Tanya Mans and her team have been quietly shaping how the Garden Route lives.

By Garden Route Living

There's a particular kind of interior design practice that doesn't advertise, doesn't chase trends, and doesn't need to explain itself. The Workshop Interiors, tucked into the corner of Main and Nelson in Knysna, is that kind of place.

For more than twenty years, Tanya Mans has been building something rare in the design world: a business based almost entirely on reputation. Walk into any beautifully finished home on the Garden Route—from the forest estates above Knysna to the clifftop properties of Plett—and there's a reasonable chance The Workshop had a hand in it.

The name tells you something. This isn't a showroom that exists to display; it's a workshop that exists to make. Behind the fabric samples and the furniture on the floor, there's a workroom where curtains are sewn, upholstery is done, and custom pieces come together. That integration of design and craft—the ability to conceive something and then actually build it—is increasingly rare.

The Coastal Challenge

Interior design on the Garden Route presents problems that designers in Johannesburg or Cape Town never encounter. The salt air corrodes. The humidity warps. The UV light fades fabrics in months rather than years. The famous southeaster finds its way through the smallest gaps.

Two decades of working in this environment has given The Workshop institutional knowledge that can't be replicated. They know which fabrics will survive a beachfront lounge. They know which timber moves least in our humidity. They know the suppliers who actually deliver, and the ones who don't.

"The best design is invisible. You shouldn't notice the curtains or the upholstery—you should just feel that the room works."

The Approach

What distinguishes The Workshop is an absence of signature style. Some designers impose a look—you can spot their work from across the room. Tanya and her team take the opposite approach: each project responds to its specific context, its clients, its views, its light.

The result is homes that feel inevitable rather than designed. Spaces where the ocean view isn't competing with the furniture for attention. Rooms that work equally well for a quiet morning coffee and a dinner party for twenty.

Beyond Design

The Workshop offers the full spectrum: curtains and blinds, upholstery and reupholstery, furniture selection and custom pieces, full interior design and project management. But they're equally happy to simply recover a beloved chair or source a particular fabric.

That flexibility—the willingness to do small jobs as carefully as large ones—is part of how they've built their reputation. In a town where word travels fast, every job matters.

Visiting

The Workshop Interiors is located at the corner of Main Road and Nelson Street in central Knysna, open weekdays from 7:30am to 5pm. No appointment necessary—though for design consultations, calling ahead is appreciated.
