Saturday at Wild Oats

For 25 years, this Sedgefield market has been the Garden Route's Saturday morning ritual.

By Garden Route Living

By 8am on a Saturday morning, the parking area at Wild Oats is already filling up. Families arrive with dogs and empty shopping bags. Cyclists rack their bikes. The smell of coffee and fresh bread drifts across the grounds.

Wild Oats Community Farmers' Market has been a Sedgefield institution since 2000. What started as a small gathering of local producers has grown into the Garden Route's most beloved weekend ritual—a place where community happens as much as commerce.

The Stalls

The market is organised around food: fresh produce from local farms, artisan bread, cheese, olives, biltong, honey, jams, and dozens of other products made within a few hours' drive. The emphasis is on local and handmade. You won't find mass-produced imports here.

Beyond food, there's a curated selection of crafts, art, and handmade goods. Quality control is strict—every stallholder must make or grow what they sell.

"It's not just a market. It's where our community comes together every week."

The Atmosphere

Live music plays throughout the morning. Children run between stalls while parents catch up with neighbours over coffee. The pace is unhurried, the vibe relaxed. This is the Garden Route at its most authentic—a community that values connection, quality, and the simple pleasures of a Saturday morning well spent.

Visiting

When: Every Saturday, 8am–12pm

Where: Sedgefield (N2, main road through town)

Dogs: Welcome on leads

Tip: Arrive early for the best selection and parking
