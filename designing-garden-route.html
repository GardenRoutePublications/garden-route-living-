Designing for the Garden Route: Where Climate Meets Craft

The science and art of creating interiors that thrive in South Africa's most demanding coastal environment.

By Garden Route Living

The Garden Route is beautiful precisely because it's wild. But that wildness—the salt air, the humidity, the fierce summer sun and winter storms—makes interior design here unlike anywhere else in South Africa.

Ask any homeowner along this 300-kilometre stretch of coastline about their biggest maintenance challenge, and you'll hear the same answer: windows. The very views that drew them here become their greatest vulnerability—to UV damage, to moisture, to the relentless coastal wind.

We spoke with Custom Blinds Shutters & Awnings, the Garden Route's leading specialists in window treatments, about what it really takes to design for this unique environment.

Understanding the Coastal Challenge

"Most people think choosing blinds is purely aesthetic," says the team at Custom Blinds, who have completed over 8,000 installations across George, Knysna, Plettenberg Bay, Mossel Bay, Sedgefield and Wilderness. "But here on the Garden Route, it's actually an engineering decision first."

The challenges are multifaceted. Salt air corrodes metal components within months if they're not marine-grade. Humidity can warp wooden shutters that haven't been properly sealed. UV radiation fades fabrics and degrades plastics faster than anywhere inland. And the famous Garden Route winds can tear poorly secured awnings from their mountings.

"Here on the Garden Route, choosing window treatments isn't purely aesthetic—it's an engineering decision first."

Room by Room: What Works Where

Living Areas with Ocean Views

For the dramatic floor-to-ceiling windows that define Garden Route architecture, Custom Blinds typically recommends their Custom Lifestyle™ range of outdoor blinds. These create seamless indoor-outdoor flow while protecting interiors from the elements.

Bedrooms

Blackout blinds are essential for bedrooms, particularly in summer when the sun rises before 5am. The company's blockout roller blinds, combined with a pelmet for complete light seal, ensure restful sleep year-round.

Bathrooms and Kitchens

Moisture-resistant materials are non-negotiable in these high-humidity spaces. Aluminium Venetian blinds or PVC shutters resist warping and mould, maintaining their appearance through years of steam and splashes.

Outdoor Entertainment Areas

The Garden Route's outdoor living culture demands solutions that extend usable space. Canvas awnings provide shade while allowing airflow, and motorised outdoor blinds can transform an exposed stoep into a year-round room at the touch of a button.

The Smart Home Revolution
